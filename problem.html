The Problem

Climate change is one of the most monumental challenges facing mankind today. It is listed as one of the United Nations 17 development goals. To combat the problem, they specify the need for improving education, awareness-raising and human and institutional capacity on climate change mitigation, adaptation, impact reduction and early warning.

Facts on expected climate change

  • From 1880 to 2012, average global temperature increased by 0.85°C
  • Oceans have warmed, the amounts of snow and ice have diminished and sea level has risen. From 1901 to 2010, the global average sea level rose by 19 cm as oceans expanded due to warming and ice melted.
  • Global emissions of carbon dioxide (CO2) have increased by almost 50 per cent since 1990
  • (Source: Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change)

Over the past years we have seen a surge in content from literature, celebrities & influencers, internet communities regarding this question. One may think that this has made us well informed, but most people are still not aware of two essentials:

  • What is my carbon footprint?
  • What are the optimal measures to reduce it?
